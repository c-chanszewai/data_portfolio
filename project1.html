Short-term Rental Homes In Hong Kong
(LibreOffice Calc + Tableau)

I was interested in this project because I was planning a month-long stay and wanted to decide between renting a serviced room in a hotel or an Airbnb.


I obtained and combined three datasets from the Airbnb website, updated in June 2024. After cleaning the data using LibreOffice Calc, I used Tableau to create interactive visualizations to compare prices and ratings across different districts. However, I realized that, given Hong Kong's small size and Airbnb being just one of many accommodation options, the data might not be representative in certain areas. To address this, I included the number of rentals and reviews in the comparisons. The final visualization provides a detailed view of each individual rental.


Half of the rental homes are located in Yau Tsim Mong, with an average price of $668 per night. Wan Chai, Central & Western, and several outlying islands each account for around 10% of rentals, with average prices of $732, $1,004, and $988, respectively. Interestingly, average review scores tend to increase along with rental prices. Specialty rentals, typically priced higher, are available across multiple districts.


The older buildings in Yau Tsim Mong and Wan Chai make it possible to convert apartments into budget-friendly, short-term rentals, while vacation homes on the outlying islands could also be a viable business option. For short-term stays, though, I’d personally prefer hotels—the abundance of high-rise hotels in Hong Kong means that they tend to offer better value if you're not specifically after the 'local living' experience.
